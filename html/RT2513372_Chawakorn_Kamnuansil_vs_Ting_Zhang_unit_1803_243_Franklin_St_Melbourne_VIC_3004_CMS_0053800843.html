Unfortunately, I have not had a response from the other party indicating that they would be willing to participate in the early resolution process. The next step then may be to progress this matter to a hearing.

 

I will advise that the applicant is required to pay an application fee for your upcoming VCAT hearing.

PLEASE NOTE: If we do not receive the application fee by 5th February 2026, we will not progress your application to hearing and it may be struck out. 

Once payment has been completed, I will send information regarding documents required for hearing. 

How to pay your hearing fee

There are three payment options: 

  • Online: my.rdrv.vic.gov.au/rtpayments/?reference=PAY-K9X1Z8-R8N8C1
  • By phone on 1300 01 8228
  • In person on the ground floor at 55 King Street, Melbourne 3000. Payment methods: cash, EFTPOS or credit card.

 

Fee relief and fee waivers

The Principal Registrar has the power to waive or reduce a daily hearing fee in certain circumstances. To find out about whether you are eligible and to apply go to: www.vcat.vic.gov.au/steps-to-resolve-your-case/fees-at-vcat/fee-relief

Any application to waive or reduce the daily hearing fee should be made as soon as possible in order to avoid any delay in the hearing.

If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to ask.
